# The Investigative Knowledge Base Playbook: Traceable, Falsifiable, Self-Revising Corpora for AI Investigative Agents

## TL;DR
- **Build the corpus as a claim-centric, append-only knowledge graph where every claim is a first-class object** carrying its own provenance chain (source → agent/run → evidence → reasoning), an explicit epistemic status, a calibrated confidence score, and a bi-temporal validity window — then never delete, only supersede, so the full history of "what was believed when and why" survives. The proven reference pattern is the nanopublication triad (assertion / provenance / publication-info) layered on RDF-star + named graphs, given lifecycle mechanics by a Truth-Maintenance-System-style dependency network and Graphiti/Zep-style edge invalidation.
- **Treat conflict and retraction as normal operations, not exceptions.** Detect-classify-resolve-generate pipelines (with source-credibility weighting and multi-perspective retention) handle live disputes; AGM belief revision, Truth/Assumption-based Maintenance Systems, and Wikidata-style rank demotion handle invalidation. The dominant failure mode to engineer against is *silent resolution* — an agent quietly picking one source and discarding the disagreement.
- **Separate gathering from deciding.** Read-only investigator sub-agents produce candidate claims with evidence; a lead/curator agent and adversarial verifier agents promote, dispute, or retract them against explicit thresholds. This orchestrator-worker split is validated by Anthropic's multi-agent research system: per Anthropic's engineering post "How we built our multi-agent research system" (June 2025), "a multi-agent system with Claude Opus 4 as the lead agent and Claude Sonnet 4 subagents outperformed single-agent Claude Opus 4 by 90.2% on our internal research eval" — the pattern is what lets the corpus grow continuously without agents overwriting each other's decisions.

## Key Findings
1. **A claim must be a structured object, not a sentence.** The most transferable schema in the wild is the **nanopublication** (Groth et al., 2010): an *assertion* graph (the claim), a *provenance* graph (how it was derived — methods, sources, evidence), and a *publication-info* graph (who/what/when created it). Nanopublications are explicitly **immutable — "once published, nanopublications cannot be changed (only retracted or superseded)"** — which is exactly the traceability guarantee an investigative corpus needs.
2. **Provenance has a mature standard: W3C PROV-O** (Entity / Activity / Agent, with derivation and attribution relations). Statement-level provenance (per-claim, not per-document) is best carried with **RDF-star** or **named graphs**, which let you attach certainty scores, timestamps, and source to a single triple.
3. **Falsifiability is best operationalized through argumentation structure.** The **Toulmin model** (claim / grounds / warrant / backing / qualifier / rebuttal) gives each claim an explicit place for the evidence, the inferential warrant, *and* the rebuttal conditions. **Dung's abstract argumentation frameworks** and **defeasible logic** formalize how claims *attack* and *defend* each other, and which set of claims can be collectively held ("extensions") once attacks are resolved.
4. **Confidence needs both a scale and calibration discipline.** Adopt an ordinal epistemic-status vocabulary (analogous to Wikidata rank and the US Intelligence Community's ICD 203 likelihood/confidence scales) *plus* numeric scores — but treat LLM self-reported confidence as **systematically overconfident** and calibrate it against agreement signals (self-consistency vote share, multi-agent agreement) rather than trusting a single verbalized number.
5. **Conflict handling is a solved-enough problem with a clear taxonomy.** The literature distinguishes **context-memory, inter-context, and intra-memory** conflicts (Xu et al. survey, arXiv:2403.08319) and, for retrieved evidence, **no-conflict / complementary / conflicting-opinions / outdated / misinformation** (DRAGged into Conflicts, arXiv:2506.08500). The empirically validated intervention: **explicitly prompt the model to reason about the conflict type before answering.**
6. **Invalidation/belief-revision has 40+ years of formal theory to borrow.** Classical **Truth Maintenance Systems (Doyle 1979)** and **Assumption-based TMS (de Kleer 1986)** track *justifications* so that when a premise is retracted, everything depending on it is automatically re-labeled. **AGM belief revision** (expansion/contraction/revision, epistemic entrenchment, the Levi identity) prescribes *minimal, consistent* change. Modern agent-memory systems (**Graphiti/Zep**, arXiv:2501.13956) implement the practical version: bi-temporal edges that are **invalidated, not deleted**.
7. **Continuous investigation needs an orchestrator-worker loop with external memory and stopping criteria.** NELL (the Never-Ending Language Learner, CMU, running since January 2010) is the canonical proof that a confidence-weighted, continuously-growing, self-retraining KB is feasible over years — accumulating 100M+ confidence-weighted beliefs — and its EM-style loop even *unlearns* previously-accepted beliefs, the exact retraction behavior required.

## Details

### Category A — Corpus Architecture & Provenance

**The claim as first-class citizen.** Every atomic finding should be stored as a structured claim record, not free text. Borrow the **nanopublication** triad (Groth et al., 2010): the *assertion* (the claim itself, ideally as a subject-predicate-object triple or a normalized natural-language proposition), the *provenance* (evidence, method, source documents, the extracting agent), and the *publication info* (creation time, agent/run ID, license, version). Nanopublications are RDF named graphs and are designed to be **citable at the granularity of a single claim** and **immutable — retracted or superseded rather than edited**. This directly satisfies the requirement to preserve "what was believed when." An extension worth noting adds a fourth **knowledge-provenance** graph for assertions derived not from one source but from a body of supporting *and conflicting* evidence — directly relevant to an investigative corpus (CEUR-WS Vol-3937).

**Provenance graph model.** Use **W3C PROV-O** as the backbone: a claim is an `Entity` `wasGeneratedBy` an investigation `Activity` `wasAssociatedWith` an `Agent` (a specific sub-agent run), and `wasDerivedFrom` source entities. PROV-O is domain-agnostic and widely adopted for **data lineage**, so it aligns the corpus with standard data-engineering tooling. For per-claim metadata (confidence, timestamps, source-credibility), plain RDF cannot natively annotate a single triple; the two mature solutions are **RDF-star** (embed a triple as the subject of metadata triples — e.g., `<< :X :hasProp :Y >> :certainty 0.9`) and **named graphs** (wrap each statement in its own graph and describe that graph). RDF-star is the more compact and increasingly tool-supported option (GraphDB, RDF4J); the "Easy and complex" work (arXiv:2211.16195) argues RDF-star + named graphs together are the viable meta-level solution.

**Versioning / audit-log discipline.** Model updates the way **Wikidata** and **git** do: never mutate in place. Wikidata keeps every prior value with `start time`/`end time` qualifiers for genuinely historical facts, and demotes erroneous ones to **deprecated rank** with a **`reason for deprecated rank` (P2241)** qualifier — a directly reusable pattern. Per Wikidata's own Help:Ranking, "The deprecated rank is used for statements that are known to include errors … or that represent outdated knowledge." A git-like content-addressed revision history (each claim revision is an immutable commit referencing its parent) gives you blame/diff/rollback over the corpus. **Data-lineage** concepts (upstream/downstream dependency tracking) let you answer "if this source is discredited, which downstream claims are affected?"

**Recommended storage granularity.** Claim node + separate evidence nodes + source nodes + agent-run nodes, all edges timestamped. Bidirectional indices (claim↔source, claim↔agent-run) — as in Graphiti's "non-lossy episodic subgraph," which lets "semantic artifacts … be traced to their sources for citation or quotation" — enable traversal from any claim to its originating evidence *and* from any source to everything it supports (critical for cascading retraction).

### Category B — Falsifiability & Claim Structuring

**Make every claim checkable by construction.** A claim that cannot in principle be disproven should not be admitted. Operationalize Popperian falsifiability with a **Toulmin-style schema** on each claim: `claim`, `grounds` (data/evidence), `warrant` (why the grounds support the claim), `backing` (support for the warrant), `qualifier` (the confidence hedge — "likely," "high confidence"), and — the key falsifiability field — **`rebuttal`/`defeater` conditions** ("this claim would be false if X"). In Toulmin's own scheme, claim, grounds, and warrant are the three essential components; backing, qualifier, and rebuttal are the supplementary ones that make an argument testable. Storing the rebuttal condition explicitly turns a bare assertion into a testable hypothesis and gives verifier agents a concrete target.

**Argumentation frameworks for structured dispute.** **Dung's abstract argumentation frameworks** (arguments + a binary *attack* relation; Dung 1995, "On the Acceptability of Arguments…") provide the formal semantics for "which claims can be jointly believed" via *extensions* (grounded, preferred, stable). **Structured/ASPIC-style** argumentation (Prakken et al.) refines attacks into **undermining** (attack a premise), **rebutting** (attack a conclusion), and **undercutting** (attack an inference), and uses **preferences** to decide which attack succeeds — a natural fit for source-credibility weighting. **Defeasible logic** lets rules be defeated by exceptions without global inconsistency, which is what non-monotonic real-world evidence demands.

**Adversarial verification as the falsification engine.** Falsifiability is meaningless without an active attempt to falsify. Borrow the internal playbook's **Chain-of-Verification** (draft claim → generate verification questions → answer them in a *fresh context* so hallucinations aren't copied → drop unverified claims) and **verification sub-agent / "second opinion"** patterns, and add explicit **red-team/adversarial verifier agents** whose job is to construct the strongest attack on each high-stakes claim. Multi-agent debate (Du et al., 2023, "Improving Factuality and Reasoning in Language Models through Multiagent Debate") measurably reduces hallucination by forcing disagreements to be argued out — but see the failure modes on sycophancy/conformity below.

### Category C — Certainty / Confidence Representation

**Dual representation: ordinal status + numeric score.** Give each claim an **epistemic status tag** from a controlled vocabulary — e.g., `hypothesized → probable → corroborated → confirmed → disputed → superseded → retracted` — analogous to **Wikidata rank** (preferred/normal/deprecated) and the **Intelligence Community's ICD 203** standards. Per ODNI Intelligence Community Directive 203 (Analytic Standards, amended 2015), the mandated likelihood terms map to a seven-band ladder: "almost no chance 01–05%, very unlikely 05–20%, unlikely 20–45%, roughly even chance 45–55%, likely 55–80%, very likely 80–95%, almost certain 95–99%." *Separately*, ICD 203 defines a High/Moderate/Low **analytic confidence** rating reflecting source quality and corroboration, and instructs that products "must not combine a confidence level and a degree of likelihood, which refers to an event or development, in the same sentence." This clean separation of "how probable is the claim" from "how good is our evidence for that probability" is directly transferable and should be enforced in the schema as **two fields, not one**.

**Calibration is non-negotiable.** LLM verbalized confidence is **systematically overconfident**. Zhao et al., "Wired for Overconfidence: A Mechanistic Perspective on Inflated Verbalized Confidence in LLMs" (arXiv:2604.01457), find that LLMs "are often not just wrong, but confidently wrong … systematically producing inflated verbalized confidence scores," and show targeted circuit interventions can cut expected calibration error (ECE) by up to 85–97%. Independently, "On Verbalized Confidence Scores for LLMs" (arXiv:2412.14737) reports that "among the evaluated LLMs with at least 70 billion parameters, the ECE is around 0.1" — i.e., confidence deviates from true accuracy by ~10 percentage points even at scale (a model- and domain-dependent figure). Therefore:
- Prefer **agreement-derived confidence** (Self-Consistency vote share over K reasoning paths; multi-agent agreement; cross-source corroboration count) over a single self-reported number.
- Use **adaptive self-consistency** to spend more samples only where the vote is split.
- Recalibrate verbalized scores against held-out accuracy before storing them as the claim's confidence.

**Uncertainty propagation.** When a claim's warrant depends on other claims, its confidence must be a *function* of its parents' confidences (a claim resting on a 0.6 premise cannot itself be 0.95). Bayesian/possibilistic extensions to TMS (D'Ambrosio 1987, "Truth maintenance with numeric certainty estimates"; Dubois-Lang-Prade possibilistic ATMS) and Bayesian belief networks provide the formal machinery; the ATMS's assumption-labeling is the lightweight version — each derived claim carries the set of base assumptions it depends on, so its confidence and its validity both track those assumptions.

### Category D — Conflict Reconciliation

**Classify before resolving.** Two complementary taxonomies:
- **Xu et al., "Knowledge Conflicts for LLMs: A Survey" (arXiv:2403.08319)** defines **context-memory** (retrieved evidence vs. the model's parametric memory — driven by temporal misalignment and misinformation pollution), **inter-context** (retrieved sources disagree with each other), and **intra-memory** (the model contradicts itself across paraphrases) conflicts. Its central recommendation, verbatim: *"Blindly being 'faithful' to context or knowledge is undesirable"* — disentangle the sources (e.g., DisentQA-style separate context-based and parameter-based answers) and give the consumer both.
- **Cattan et al., "DRAGged into Conflicts" (arXiv:2506.08500)** classifies retrieved-evidence situations into **no-conflict, complementary, conflicting opinions/research outcomes, outdated (freshness), and misinformation**, and prescribes a *different expected behavior per type*: consolidate complementary information without framing it as a debate; neutrally summarize both sides for opinion conflicts; prioritize up-to-date information for freshness; disregard misinformation. Its CONFLICTS benchmark has 458 expert-annotated instances, ~65% conflicting. Its headline finding, verbatim: *"prompting LLMs to explicitly reason about the potential conflict in the retrieved documents significantly improves the quality and appropriateness of their responses."* (Note: genuine misinformation was rare "in the wild" — only 5 of 458 cases.)

**Detect-classify-resolve-generate pipeline.** The named pattern (e.g., ConflictRAG, arXiv:2605.17301 — a very recent, apparently unrefereed preprint whose ID/date look anomalous, so treat its metrics as self-reported) runs: (1) **detect** conflict (cheap encoder classifier, escalating to an LLM only when confidence < threshold, e.g. 0.7); (2) **classify** the type (no-conflict/factual/temporal/opinion); (3) **resolve** type-adaptively — for *factual* conflicts, score sources on **authority, recency, relevance, specificity, consistency** and select (an Entropy-TOPSIS weighting found authority ~0.31 and recency ~0.25 dominant); for *temporal*, prefer the latest; for *opinion*, **synthesize multiple perspectives with attribution**; (4) **generate** a response with conflict annotations, source attribution, and a confidence qualifier. Peer-reviewed adjacent systems worth citing instead/alongside: **FaithfulRAG** (fact-level conflict modeling, arXiv:2506.08938), **Wang et al., "Retrieval-Augmented Generation with Conflicting Evidence"** (arXiv:2504.13079, a multi-agent-debate approach), and **TruthfulRAG** (knowledge-graph + entropy-based resolution, arXiv:2511.10375). Foundational behavior studies: Xie et al., "Adaptive Chameleon or Stubborn Sloth" (ConflictQA, ICLR 2024), showing LLMs tend to follow context regardless of correctness; and Longpre et al., "Entity-Based Knowledge Conflicts in Question Answering" (EMNLP 2021).

**Source-credibility weighting via truth discovery.** Classical **truth-discovery** algorithms — TruthFinder (Yin et al.); the Bayesian model of Zhao et al. (arXiv:1203.0058); the CRH optimization framework (Li et al., SIGMOD 2014, "Resolving conflicts in heterogeneous data by truth discovery and source reliability estimation") — jointly estimate *source reliability* and *the truth* in an unsupervised loop: sources that agree with the emerging consensus gain weight, and high-weight sources pull the consensus. CRH frames this as minimizing "the overall weighted deviation between the truths and the multi-source observations where each source is weighted by its reliability." This is the principled alternative to the internal playbook's noted failure of **over-trusting SEO-optimized / high-ranked sources** over authoritative ones.

**Multi-perspective retention — don't force premature resolution.** For live disputes (subjective questions, unsettled research), the corpus should retain **both sides as co-existing claims** linked by a `conflictsWith` / `Contested-Claim` edge and a `Resolution-Thread`, exactly as proposed in agent-native scientific-publishing designs (Traxia, arXiv:2606.08256, which flags contested claims and opens a resolution thread when pairwise contradiction is detected) and as GoT-style **branch merging** enables. This maps to Dung's framework: keep both arguments in the graph; only collapse to one when an attack decisively succeeds.

### Category E — Fact Invalidation & Belief Revision

**The dependency-tracking core (TMS/ATMS).** A **Truth Maintenance System** (Doyle 1979, "A truth maintenance system," *Artificial Intelligence* 12) records *justifications* linking beliefs to their supports; when a support is retracted, the TMS automatically re-derives which conclusions remain IN vs. OUT. An **Assumption-based TMS** (de Kleer 1986, "An assumption-based truth maintenance system," *Artificial Intelligence* 28:127–162) labels each belief with the minimal set of assumptions it depends on and maintains *multiple consistent contexts* simultaneously — perfect for holding rival hypotheses without global inconsistency. For an investigative KB this means: when a source is discredited or a claim retracted, every downstream claim is automatically flagged for re-evaluation via its recorded justification chain, and nothing has to be found by manual search.

**The normative theory (AGM).** **AGM belief revision** (Alchourrón, Gärdenfors & Makinson, 1985, "On the Logic of Theory Change," *Journal of Symbolic Logic*) defines three operations — **expansion** (add a belief), **contraction** (remove one), and **revision** (add while restoring consistency) — connected by the **Levi identity** (revise = contract the negation, then expand). Its guiding principle is **minimal change**: give up as little as possible, and when forced to choose, discard the belief with the lowest **epistemic entrenchment** (Gärdenfors & Makinson 1988). For the KB, entrenchment ≈ a function of confidence × corroboration × source authority: low-entrenchment claims are surrendered first when a contradiction must be resolved.

**The practical implementation (bi-temporal invalidation).** **Graphiti/Zep** (Rasmussen et al., "Zep: A Temporal Knowledge Graph Architecture for Agent Memory," arXiv:2501.13956) is the state-of-the-art engineering realization: every edge/fact carries **four timestamps** — `t_valid`/`t_invalid` (when the fact was true in the world) and `t_created`/`t_expired` (when the system learned/retired it). When new information contradicts an existing fact, "it invalidates the affected edges by setting their `t_invalid` to the `t_valid` of the invalidating edge" — the old edge is **invalidated, not deleted**, preserving history and enabling "what did we believe as of date D?" queries. Zep reports state-of-the-art on the Deep Memory Retrieval benchmark (94.8% with gpt-4-turbo; 98.2% with gpt-4o-mini, vs. MemGPT's 93.4%). Graphiti is the leading open-source temporal knowledge graph for agent memory (20,000+ GitHub stars) and powers Zep's Context Lake. OpenAI's "Temporal Agents with Knowledge Graphs" cookbook uses a dedicated **Invalidation Agent** for exactly this. This is the concrete answer to the retraction requirement.

**Retraction chains & supersession links.** Combine the above into explicit `supersededBy`, `retractedBy`, and `wasInvalidatedBy` edges. A retraction is itself a claim (with its own provenance and confidence) — so "why did belief change" is a first-class, queryable object. **Temporal fact-validation** research (Soulard et al., "When Facts Expire," CIKM 2025; TemporalFC) shows why: a large share of real-world facts carry temporal constraints, so "true then, false now" must be distinguished from "never true" — Wikidata makes exactly this distinction by using `start time`/`end time` for the former and `deprecated rank` for the latter.

**Non-monotonicity is the reality.** NELL demonstrates the loop in production: in its EM-style epochs it re-estimates every belief's probability and can, per the CMU/arXiv description, "unlearn" statements accepted in previous rounds — and re-learn them later. NELL requires a confidence score of at least 0.9 to *promote* a candidate to a believed statement, and periodic human review (roughly every few weeks) arrests semantic drift (precision rose from ~71% to ~87% after human feedback was added). An investigative KB must expect and log exactly this oscillation rather than treating any promotion as permanent.

### Category F — Orchestration for Continuous Investigation

**Orchestrator-worker with read-only investigators.** Adopt the **Anthropic multi-agent research** pattern: a **lead/orchestrator** decomposes the investigation, spawns parallel **read-only sub-agents** each in its own context window, and reconciles their **condensed findings** (~1–2k tokens each) — the design that, per Anthropic's June 2025 engineering post, beat single-agent Claude Opus 4 by **90.2%** on their internal research eval (at a cost of ~15× the tokens of a normal chat, with token usage explaining ~80% of BrowseComp performance variance). Keep gathering separate from deciding (the Cognition/Anthropic reconciliation): investigators propose *candidate* claims with evidence; only the lead + verifier roles promote, dispute, or retract them. A dedicated **CitationAgent** maps each promoted claim to its exact source location.

**Agent roles for this KB (concrete):**
- **Investigator (read-only, parallel):** gather evidence, emit candidate claims in the Toulmin+nanopub schema with self-consistency-derived confidence.
- **Verifier / Red-team (adversarial):** run Chain-of-Verification in fresh context; attempt to falsify; attach rebuttals.
- **Reconciler:** detect/classify/resolve conflicts; maintain `conflictsWith` edges and multi-perspective retention.
- **Curator / Belief-Revision agent:** apply promotion thresholds, AGM-style minimal revision, bi-temporal invalidation, and retraction-chain maintenance.
- **Citation agent:** enforce claim↔source mapping; LLM-as-judge scores citation accuracy.
- **Lead/Orchestrator:** plans, allocates, holds all final decision authority, writes plan to external memory.

**External memory & compaction.** Because investigations are long-horizon, persist state outside the context window: a **NOTES.md-style scratchpad** / the claim graph itself is the durable memory; the orchestrator **saves its plan to memory before context fills** (Anthropic note the ~200k-token truncation risk, and that subagents should write to a shared store and return lightweight references rather than long chat-style payloads). Use **compaction** (summarize history, recall-first-then-precision) to survive resets without losing decisions.

**Stopping / convergence criteria.** Define explicit early-stop rules: stop investigating a sub-question when new retrievals stop being novel (information plateau), when the confidence of the target claim crosses a threshold, or when a **sufficient-context** check passes. For debate/verification loops, a **sequential-probability-ratio (Wald SPRT)** governor (Morandi, arXiv:2605.19193) can stop as soon as the consensus signal is informative enough, avoiding wasted compute.

### Cross-cutting Failure Modes to Engineer Against
- **Silent resolution:** an agent picks one source and drops the conflict without flagging it. Mitigation: mandatory conflict-detection pass; `conflictsWith` edges are required output, not optional.
- **Overconfidence / miscalibration:** verbalized confidence inflated. Mitigation: agreement-derived confidence + recalibration; never store a raw self-reported number as ground truth.
- **SEO / popularity bias:** ranking-by-retrievability instead of authority. Mitigation: truth-discovery source weighting; explicit authority field.
- **Sycophancy / conformity in debate:** minority-but-correct positions get suppressed ("the confident liar" wins); some studies find weak models correct only ~3.6% of stance biases and instead conform to the majority. Mitigation: heterogeneous debaters, log-probability/LLM-as-judge diagnostics, minority-sentinel checks, preserve dissent in the graph.
- **Hallucination copying in verification:** verifier sees and parrots the draft. Mitigation: factored CoVe — verify in a *fresh, draft-blind* context.
- **Cascade-on-retraction missed:** discrediting a source leaves orphaned downstream claims believed. Mitigation: TMS/ATMS justification chains + data-lineage downstream traversal.
- **Runaway cost:** recursive sub-agent spawning, no circuit breakers. Mitigation: per-run caps, effort-scaling rules in the orchestrator prompt.
- **Semantic drift over long horizons:** NELL-style degradation. Mitigation: periodic human review checkpoints; coupled/consistency constraints.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Schematize the claim (do this first).** Define a single claim record combining nanopublication structure and Toulmin fields:
```
Claim {
  id, assertion (triple + normalized NL),
  grounds[], warrant, backing[], rebuttal_conditions[],   // falsifiability
  epistemic_status ∈ {hypothesized,probable,corroborated,confirmed,disputed,superseded,retracted},
  likelihood_band (ICD-203 7-band ladder), analytic_confidence ∈ {high,mod,low},  // kept SEPARATE
  confidence_score (agreement-derived, calibrated),
  provenance { source[], evidence[], method, extracting_agent_run },
  t_valid, t_invalid, t_created, t_expired,               // bi-temporal
  depends_on[] (justifications → ATMS), conflictsWith[], supersededBy, retractedBy,
  revision_history[] (git-like, append-only)
}
```
Store as RDF-star or named graphs over a graph DB; index bidirectionally (claim↔source, claim↔agent-run).

**Stage 2 — Stand up the agent roles and the update loop.** Investigator(s) → Verifier/Red-team → Reconciler → Curator/Belief-Revision → Citation, coordinated by a Lead with external memory. The **update/revision loop** per cycle:
1. Investigators emit candidate claims (+ evidence, + self-consistency confidence).
2. Verifiers run factored CoVe in fresh context and attach rebuttals.
3. Reconciler runs detect→classify→resolve; opens `conflictsWith` edges; retains multi-perspective for live disputes.
4. Curator promotes claims crossing threshold, applies AGM minimal revision on contradiction, sets bi-temporal invalidation + supersession/retraction chains, cascades re-evaluation via justification links.
5. Citation agent finalizes source mapping; Lead compacts and checkpoints.

**Stage 3 — Wire in the guardrails.** Mandatory conflict-flagging (kill silent resolution); agreement-based calibration; truth-discovery source weighting; adversarial verification on high-stakes claims; per-run cost caps; periodic human-review checkpoints to arrest semantic drift.

**Thresholds that change the plan:**
- *Promote* hypothesized→corroborated when ≥2 independent authoritative sources agree and calibrated confidence ≥ ~0.7; →confirmed at ≥~0.9 with no open rebuttals (NELL's own promotion bar is 0.9).
- *Demote/dispute* when a new contradicting claim from an equal-or-higher-authority source appears → open dispute, retain both, do not overwrite.
- *Retract/supersede* when the supporting evidence is invalidated or a decisive attack succeeds → set `t_invalid`, add `retractedBy`, cascade to dependents.
- *Stop investigating* a thread on information plateau, threshold-crossing, or passing a sufficient-context check; use Wald SPRT for debate loops.
- *Escalate to human* when confidence is high but calibration disagrees, when a high-authority source is contradicted, or when misinformation is suspected.

**Build-vs-borrow:** Use **Graphiti** (open-source, bi-temporal, 20,000+ GitHub stars) as the invalidation/temporal substrate; **PROV-O + RDF-star/nanopublications** for the provenance/versioning layer; a **TMS/ATMS** library or equivalent justification-tracking for cascade retraction; the **DRAGged-into-Conflicts** taxonomy + a detect-classify-resolve pipeline (FaithfulRAG / RAG-with-Conflicting-Evidence as peer-reviewed anchors) for reconciliation; and the **Anthropic orchestrator-worker** pattern for the agent layer.

## Caveats
- **Formal frameworks vs. LLM reality.** AGM, TMS/ATMS, and Dung's frameworks assume clean logical consistency and discrete beliefs; LLM-extracted claims are noisy, probabilistic, and natural-language. These frameworks are best used as *design scaffolding* and for the *high-stakes/structured* subset of claims, not as a literal logic engine over the entire corpus.
- **Confidence numbers are soft.** Even calibrated, LLM confidence and self-consistency vote share are proxies, not probabilities; the ~0.1 ECE figure is model- and domain-dependent (measured on 70B+ models in arXiv:2412.14737, not a universal constant). Treat all stored scores as provisional and re-calibrate periodically.
- **Multi-agent debate is not a free lunch.** Recent work shows gains can shrink under stronger single-agent prompting and that conformity/sycophancy can suppress correct minority views — debate must be engineered (heterogeneous agents, dissent preservation, minority-sentinel checks), not assumed to work.
- **Source anomaly flag.** The "ConflictRAG" preprint (arXiv:2605.17301) named in internal references has an anomalous ID/date and appears unrefereed; its pipeline concept is sound and mirrored by peer-reviewed systems, but its specific metrics should not be cited as established. Several other very recent arXiv IDs surfaced (2025–2026 numbering) are likewise pre-publication and should be verified before formal citation.
- **Cost and scale.** A full multi-agent, multi-verifier loop with per-claim provenance is expensive (Anthropic report single research sessions using millions of tokens at ~15× normal chat cost, with no built-in circuit breakers); the architecture is justified for high-value investigative domains (due diligence, competitive/intelligence analysis, biomedical literature synthesis), not consumer Q&A.
- **Human-in-the-loop remains necessary.** NELL, Wikidata, and IC practice all retain periodic human review; fully autonomous long-horizon KBs drift (NELL's precision degraded on some relations until human feedback was added). Budget for review checkpoints as a permanent component, not a temporary crutch.
